Payments you may receive from us

By filing a tax return, you may be eligible for one or more of the following payments:

GST/HST Credit

Provides four tax-free payments per year. If eligible, single individuals can get up to $496 per year. A couple with two children could get up to $992 per year.

If you qualify for GSTC/HSTC, you automatically qualify for certain provincial or territorial payments.

Based on where you live, you may also get the:

  • BC climate action tax credit
  • New Brunswick harmonized sales tax credit
  • Newfoundland and Labrador income supplement
  • Newfoundland and Labrador disability amount
  • Newfoundland and Labrador seniors' benefit
  • Northwest Territories cost of living offset
  • Nova Scotia affordable living tax credit
  • Nunavut carbon credit
  • Prince Edward Island sales tax credit
  • Saskatchewan low-income tax credit (SLITC)
  • Yukon Government carbon price rebate - Individuals

Canada child benefit (CCB)

Provides monthly tax-free money. Eligible families can get up to $7,437 per year for each child under 6 and up to $6,275 per year, for each child aged 6 to 17.

If you qualify for CCB, you automatically qualify for certain provincial or territorial payments.

Based on where you live, you may also get the:

  • Alberta child and family benefit
  • BC family benefit
  • New Brunswick child tax benefit
  • Newfoundland and Labrador child benefit (and pre-natal infant nutrition supplement)
  • Northwest Territories child benefit
  • Nova Scotia child benefit
  • Nunavut child benefit
  • Ontario child benefit (related to the Canada child benefit)
  • Yukon child benefit

Canada workers benefit (CWB)

An amount that helps those working and earning a low income to reduce any taxes owed and can result in a tax refund

Climate action incentive payment

A quarterly payment for residents of Alberta, Saskatchewan, Manitoba and Ontario to offset the cost of the federal pollution pricing

Child disability benefit (CDB)

A tax-free monthly payment for families who care for a child under age 18 who has a severe impairment

Canada Dental Benefit

An interim tax-free payment for eligible families with children under 12 who do not have private dental insurance

Ontario trillium benefit (OTB)

A monthly payment that combines the Ontario and property tax credit, the Northern Ontario energy credit and the Ontario sales tax credit

Ontario senior homeowners' property tax grant

A grant to help offset property taxes for seniors who own their own home and have low to moderate incomes

Excise gasoline tax refund

A refund of part of the federal excise tax you pay on gasoline for those individuals who have a permanent mobility impairment and cannot safely use public transportation

Personal tax refunds

A payment for individual taxpayers who have a refund due on their Notice of assessment or reassessment

Disability Tax Credit (DTC)

Although DTC is not a payment, it is a line item on your tax return, being approved to claim the DTC can result in payments from other benefit programs

If you qualify for DTC, you may qualify for certain payments or entitlements.

Payments:

  • Canada workers benefit disability supplement
  • Child disability benefit

Deductions:

  • Canada caregiver credit
  • Home accessibility tax credit
  • Multigenerational home renovation tax credit

Savings:

  • Registered disability savings plan

  • To continue getting payments, be sure to:
    • File your income tax and benefit return on time every year, even if you have no income in the year. If you have a spouse or common-law partner, they also have to file a return every year.
    • Keep your personal information updated with the CRA
    • Keep your supporting documents in case the CRA asks for them as part of the validation process
