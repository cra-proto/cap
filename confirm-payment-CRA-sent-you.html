Payments the CRA sends you

Confirm a payment you received

You can confirm if the CRA sent you a payment by checking your online account.

On this page

  • Check your CRA account
  • Resolve an issue with a payment
  • Make sure it's really the CRA contacting you

Check your CRA account

You or your representative can sign in to your online account to see what type of payment CRA has made to you. You can see payments we have sent in the accounts and balances section, and you can find explanations on notices of assessment, reassessment, and other payment-related letters in your account if you have registered for electronic mail.

  • Individuals

    In My Account select "Accounts and payments".

  • Businesses

    In My Business Account select "View and pay account balance".

CRA sign in

If you cannot access an online account, you or your representative can contact us by phone.

Resolve an issue with a payment

If you had a debt on your account, we may have automatically used your refund, benefit or credit to pay down the debt: How we automatically apply refunds and credits to your debt

Contact us if:

  • You have been sent a payment but you have not received it after:
    • 3 business days for online payments
    • 10 business days (plus mailing time) for payments by cheque
  • You were not sent a payment you were expecting, and could not find an explanation online or in a notice we sent you

  • Individuals

    For an individual tax or benefit account:

    • Canadian residents: 1-800-959-8281 (opens up phone application)1-800-959-8281
    • Non-residents
      • Canada or U.S.: 1-855-284-5946 (opens up phone application)1-855-284-5946
      • Outside Canada or U.S.: 1-613-940-8499 (opens up phone application)1-613-940-8499

  • Businesses

    For a business account:

    • Call 1-800-959-5525 (opens up phone application)1-800-959-5525

When calling the CRA to resolve payment issues, you or your representative will need to provide additional information, such as details from one of your assessed tax returns, to verify your identity and ensure confidentiality.

Make sure it's really the CRA contacting you

If someone contacts you to say you need to take action for the CRA to send you money, confirm whether or not this is true. Do not provide any personal information without confirming it's really the CRA contacting you.

For more about what a CRA agent will and will not do when they contact you, refer to: What to expect when the CRA contacts you
