Getting debt relief under special circumstances: Payments to the CRA

Financial or extraordinary circumstances can prevent you from paying off your debt. There are options that may help you reduce the amounts you owe.

Making a full or partial payment toward your debt will reduce the interest that may be added, and doesn't prevent you from asking for relief:

  • Payment options for the type of payment you’re making
  • Arrange to pay your debt over time

  • Cancel or waive interest or penalties

    You may ask for relief from penalties and interest if you are in extraordinary circumstances or financial hardship. You may also apply if they are a result of our actions.

    Applies to:

    • CRA taxes
    • Business GST/HST and payroll
    • COVID-19 business subsidies
    • Benefits paid by the CRA
    • Employment and Social Development Canada debts
    • Customs duties

    Getting penalties and interest relief from:

    • Tax or benefit debts: Option 1 of 3

      Cancel or waive penalties at the CRA

    • EI overpayments: Option 2 of 3

      Financial hardship provision:

      • For penalties, contact ESDC
      • For interest, contact the CRA at 1-800-661-5991

    • Customs duties: Option 3 of 3

      • For penalties, contact CBSA
      • For interest, contact the CRA

  • Student or apprentice loan repayment assistance

    You may qualify for reduced payments of your student or apprentice loan. Depending on your income, you may even have no payments at all.

    Applies to:

    • Canada Student Loan and Canada Apprentice Loan

    ESDC Repayment Assistance Plan

    If you have a disability, you may be eligible for more help to pay your student or apprentice loan.

    ESDC Repayment Assistance Plan for Borrowers with Disabilities

  • Change a tax return

    You may be able to claim a tax credit or deduction in a previous year tax return that could reduce what you owe.

    Applies to:

    • CRA taxes

    • Change a recent return: Option 1 of 3

      • Change your personal income tax return
      • Change a corporate income tax return

    • After the 3 year limit: Option 2 of 3

      Refund or reduce the amount payable beyond the normal three-year period

    • Change an election: Option 3 of 3

      Late, amended, or revoked elections

  • Request remission

    Remission provides full or partial relief from federal tax, interest, penalty, or other debt to the CRA. It is an avenue of last resort when relief is warranted and all other options do not apply.

    Applies to:

    • CRA taxes
    • Business GST/HST and payroll
    • COVID-19 business subsidies
    • Benefits paid by the CRA

    Request remission

    Applies to:
