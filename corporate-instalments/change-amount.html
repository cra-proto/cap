When to change your instalment amounts

You can change the amount of tax you pay by instalments throughout the year to accommodate changes to your anticipated income or credits, or if your business undergoes a [structural change].

If your tax payable will be higher than you estimated

In some cases, we may apply instalment interest if you pay less in instalments than the taxes you owed for the year.

You should consider increasing the instalment payments you are making for the current tax year if:

  • You based your instalment payments on your estimated current-year income and tax payable (instalment calculation option 1)
    • You anticipate that your tax payable will be higher than you initially estimated

    If you based your instalment payments for this year on past year amounts (instalment calculation option 2 or 3), you do not need to recalculate your instalment payments to avoid interest. However, you may consider increasing your instalment amount if you want to avoid a large balance owing when you file your return.

    If your business status changed during the year

    Some changes to your business, such as amalgamations, transfers, or rollovers, may affect the instalments you need to pay during the year.

    Refer to: Changes to your business or tax year that may affect your instalments

    How to change your instalment amounts

    To change the amount of your instalment payments, simply recalculate and pay the new amounts. You do not need to notify us of your recalculations during the year.
