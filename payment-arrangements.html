Payments to the CRA

Arrange to pay your debt over time

If you can’t pay your debt right away, you can schedule a series of payments in your online CRA account using a pre-authorized debit (PAD) agreement.

You can also call the CRA to set up a payment arrangement.

On this page

  • Schedule a series of payments
  • Change your payment arrangement
  • We may apply other federal payments you receive to your debt

Schedule a series of payments

Calculate your monthly income and expenses

Use the personal income and expense worksheet to determine what you can afford to pay on a regular basis.

Schedule your payments online or by phone

  • Online

    Schedule a series of payments in:

    • My Account
    • My Business Account
    • Represent a Client
  • By phone

    To schedule a series of payments as part of a payment arrangement, you can:

    • Call the automated TeleArrangement service
    • Speak to an agent
Online
  1. Determine if you can set it up online

    You can schedule a series of payments in My Account, My Business Account or Represent a Client for the following debts:

    My Account

    • Personal income tax

    My Business Account

    • Corporate income tax
    • Payroll deductions
    • GST/HST
    • Canada Emergency Wage Subsidy (CEWS)
    • Excise duty, excise tax or excise tax on insurance premiums
    • Air travellers security charge (ATSC)
    • Fuel charge
    • Partnerships

    Other payments not online

    • If you are trying to pay any other type of debt, including COVID-19 benefits or benefit overpayments over time, use the by phone option.
  2. Sign in to your CRA online account

    Sign in to your account and select "Proceed to pay" then "Schedule a series of payments".

    CRA sign in

    Pay on behalf of someone else

    To schedule payments on behalf of a family member or a business you represent, sign in to Represent a Client.

  3. Confirm a PAD agreement

    After you determine a payment schedule that works best for you with the calculator available in your CRA account, confirm your PAD agreement.

By phone

  1. Before you call

    When you call, you’ll need to have:

    • Social Insurance Number (or business number)
    • Full name and date of birth (or business name)
    • Complete address (or complete business address)
    • Personal (or business) account open or an assessed return, notice of assessment, reassessment, or other tax document

  2. Call the CRA to set up your payment arrangement

    • Speak to an agent

      Find the phone number to call depending on what type of debt you are paying over time: Call us if you are unable to pay

    • Automated TeleArrangement service

      For personal income tax debt only, call our automated line to make a payment arrangement.

      Telephone number: 1-866-256-1147 (opens up phone application) 1-866-256-1147

      Hours
      Dedicated hours
      Date Hours
      Monday to Friday 7 am to 10 pm (ET)

  3. Start your payment arrangement

    To start your payment arrangement, you need to make your first payment towards your debt.

    You must continue to make payments on the agreed upon dates in your payment arrangement and file all future tax returns on time. Your payment arrangement may be reviewed periodically.

    For options, go to make a payment.

Change your payment arrangement

If you need to change your payment arrangement or you are unable to make your payment, you must contact the CRA.

If you don't modify your payment arrangement before paying less than you agreed to, then the CRA may proceed with legal actions to collect what you owe. Refer to: If you don't pay what you owe

We may apply other federal payments you receive to your debt

Even if you have a payment arrangement and are making payments, we may use some benefits and other credit payments you receive to pay your debt. Refer to: How payments are applied to offset your debt
