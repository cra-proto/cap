Schedule a series of payments

Calculate your monthly income and expenses

Use the personal income and expense worksheet to determine what you can afford to pay on a regular basis.

Schedule your payments online or by phone

  • Online

    You can schedule a series of automatic withdrawals from your bank account (pre-authorized debit (PAD) payments) in:

    • My Account
    • My Business Account
    • Represent a Client (level 3 representatives for individuals only)
  • By phone

    To work with us to set up a payment arrangement for a debt you owe, you can:

    • Call the automated TeleArrangement service
    • Speak to an agent

    Once you've made an arrangement, you can use the payment method of your choice to make your payments.

Online

  1. Determine if you can schedule payments for this debt online

    You can schedule pre-authorized debit payments in My Account, My Business Account or Represent a Client for the following debts:

    My Account

    • Personal income tax

    My Business Account

    • Corporate income tax
    • Payroll deductions
    • GST/HST
    • Canada Emergency Wage Subsidy (CEWS)
    • Excise duty, excise tax or excise tax on insurance premiums
    • Air travellers security charge (ATSC)
    • Fuel charge
    • Partnerships

    Other payments not online

    • If you are trying to pay any other type of debt, including COVID-19 benefits or benefit overpayments over time, use the by phone option.

  2. Sign in to your CRA online account

    1. Sign in to your account
    2. Select "Proceed to pay"
    3. Choose "Schedule a series of payments"
    4. Fill in the fields to set up and confirm your pre-authorized withdrawals

    CRA sign in

    Pay on behalf of someone else

    Only legal representatives for individuals can schedule payments in Represent a Client.

    Business representatives are not able schedule pre-authorized debits for businesses they represent.

  3. Start your payment agreement

    Your pre-authorized debit payment agreement begins when your first scheduled payment is withdrawn from your account.

Pay by scheduled pre-authorized debit withdrawal(s) through CRA online services

By phone

  1. Before you call

    When you call, you'll need to have:

    • Social Insurance Number or business number
    • Full name and date of birth or business name
    • Complete home or business address)
    • Have your assessed return, notice of assessment, reassessment, or other tax document, or be signed in to My Account or My Business Account

  2. Call the CRA to set up your payment arrangement

    • Speak to an agent

      Find the phone number to call depending on what type of debt you are paying over time: Call us if you are unable to pay

    • Automated TeleArrangement service

      For personal income tax debt only, call our automated line to make a payment arrangement.

      Telephone number: 1-866-256-1147 (opens up phone application) 1-866-256-1147

  3. Start your payment arrangement

    To start your payment arrangement, you need to make your first payment towards your debt.

    You must continue to make payments on the agreed upon dates in your payment arrangement and file all future tax returns on time. Your payment arrangement may be reviewed periodically.

    For options on how to make your payments, go to make a payment.

Change your payment arrangement

If you need to change a payment arrangement or you are unable to make a payment, call us if you can't pay in full or on time.

If you don't modify your payment arrangement before paying less than you agreed to, then the CRA may proceed with legal actions to collect what you owe. Refer to: If you don't pay your debt

We may apply other federal payments you receive to your debt

Even if you have a payment arrangement and are making payments, we may use some benefits and other credit payments you receive to pay your debt. Refer to: How payments are applied to offset your debt
