Here is a list of other areas you may have received a payment from:

  • For ESDC:
    • Online banking

      Payments can be made online through your financial institution.

      1. Select "Employment and Social Development Canada" as the payee
      2. Enter your Social Insurance Number (SIN) as the account number
      3. If your financial institution requires 11 characters, add the letters "YY" or the numbers "00" at the end on your entry
    • By mail

      If you are unable to make your payments online, you may pay by sending a cheque.

      1. Make your cheque payable to "Receiver General for Canada"
      2. Write your client ID number on the front of your cheque
      3. Include your remittance slip with your payment – do not staple
      4. Mail your payment to the payment office indicated on the back of your statement of account

      Do not send cash by mail.

    • For non-residents

      Foreign currency payments can by made by international wire transfer. Electronic funds transfer must be made in Canadian or US currency.

Debt that is in collections

If you received a notice that your debt is in collections, and you have not already spoken to someone at the CRA, you should contact the collections call centre or your assigned collections officer before making a payment.

The CRA never accepts certain payment methods

The CRA never accepts payments by:

  • Cryptocurrency

    We do not accept any form of cryptocurrency (e.g. Bitcoin) as payment

  • Gift cards

    We do not accept any gift cards as a form of payment

  • Traveller's cheques

    We do not accept any traveller's cheques

CRA does not accept the following payment method

  • Cash through the mail

    Cash can only be used at any Canada post retail location
