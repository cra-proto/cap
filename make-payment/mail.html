Pay through the mail: Payments to the CRA

You can make a payment to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) with a cheque from your Canadian bank account.

Canada Post or other courier services may charge you service fees.

How to pay the amount you owe

Make a payment for yourself, on behalf of someone else, or for a business by mailing the CRA a cheque.

Include a remittance voucher

The CRA will need the remittance voucher included in your envelope to apply your payment to your tax account.

We accept photocopies of remittance vouchers if they are clearly legible.

If you do not have one, you can order or print a remittance voucher or a payment form.

Addressing a cheque

Include the following details on the front memo field of your cheque:

Made out to
Receiver General for Canada
Memo field
  • Calendar year for which you are paying
  • Your relevant tax number
    • Individuals: SIN, ITN, or TTN
    • Corporations: business number and program account
    • Non-residents: withholding tax account number

Mail the CRA your cheque

Choose how you'll mail us your cheque:

  • Mail your cheque

    Address your cheque to:

    Canada Revenue Agency
    PO Box 3800 STN A
    Sudbury ON P3A 0C3

  • Drop your cheque at a CRA dropbox

    You can go to one of our locations to drop off your envelope in person. Read more about CRA offices and drop box locations

Alternatively, consider paying in person at any Canada Post outlet using cash or a debit card.

Processing time

  • Considered as paid: the same or next business day (varies based on your bank or credit union)
  • Updated in your CRA account: up to 2 business days after we receive the payment. You can confirm if we received a payment..

If your payment is still not showing after 3 business days, you can resolve a payment issue.
