Pay at the counter (teller) at a bank or credit union: Payments to the CRA

You can make a payment to the CRA at banks and credit unions in Canada through direct withdrawal from your account, by debit card, cheque or money order.

How to pay the amount you owe

You can make a payment to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) by visiting your Canadian bank or credit union.

Bring a remittance voucher

Bring a remittance voucher so the bank can apply the payment to your tax account.

Banks and credit unions do not accept photocopies of remittance vouchers or any other type of payment form.

If you do not have one, order or print a remittance voucher or a payment form.

Pay at the teller

You can pay at a bank credit or union by:

  • direct withdrawal from your bank account
  • debit card
  • cheque or money order

If you are paying from an institution outside of Canada and the United States, you may need to pay by wire transfer.

Processing time

  • Considered as paid: the same day it was paid (date stamped by employee)
  • Updated in your CRA account: usually within 2 to 3 business days

You can confirm if we received a payment.

If your payment is still not showing after 2 business days, you can resolve a payment issue.
