Pay through a third-party service provider: Payments to the CRA

The CRA does not usually accept payments directly by credit card, PayPal or Interac e-Transfer. Some third-party service providers offer these methods of payments and will send your individual or business payment and remittance details online to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) for you.

PaySimply also accepts internationally issued credit cards.

Service fees will be charged by the provider.

How to pay

When you make a payment through a third-party service provider, they need information to send your payment details to the CRA for you.

What you need before you pay

You will need the following before you pay:

  • Individual payments: Option 1 of 2

    Your SIN number

  • Business payments: Option 2 of 2

    Your Business Number and program account

Once you have the information you need, visit the website of your chosen third-party service provider.

Make a payment with PaySimply

To make a payment with PaySimply:

  1. Go to PaySimply's CRA payment page
  2. Review PaySimply's third party service fees and select the type of payment you wish to make
  3. Select your payment method. Options include:
    • Credit card payment (including internationally issued credit cards)
    • PayPal money transfer (Canadian accounts only)
    • Interac e-Transfer

How long the payment takes to be received and processed

  • Considered as paid: varies based on the service provider
  • Updated in your CRA account: usually 2 to 3 business days after the payment is received. You can confirm if a payment was received.

If your payment still hasn't shown up after 3 business days, resolve a payment issue.
