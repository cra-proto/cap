Pay online with your bank or credit union: Payments to the CRA

You can make a payment or schedule future payment(s) to the CRA directly by logging in to through the online banking site or mobile banking app of most Canadian banks or credit unions.

On this page

  • What you can and can't pay
  • How to pay the amount you owe
  • Processing time

What you can and can't pay

Payments we accept online through your bank or credit union
Individuals
  • Income tax (T1) amount owing
  • Income tax (T1) instalment payments
  • Advanced Canada Workers Benefit repayment
  • Canada Child Benefit (CCB) repayment
  • Canada Dental Benefit repayment
  • COVID-19 benefit repayment (CERB, etc.)
  • GST/HST credit repayment
  • One time top-up to Canada Housing Benefit repayment
  • Part IX GST Security deposit (non-resident)
  • Part XIII withholding tax (non-resident)
  • Universal Child Care Benefit repayment
Businesses
  • Corporation income tax (T2)
  • Corporation income tax (T2) instalment payments
  • GST/HST remittances
  • Source deductions (payroll)
  • Air Travellers Security Charge
  • COVID-19 business subsidy overpayments (CEWS, etc.) except rent subsidy
  • Excise duties
  • Excise tax
  • Excise tax on insurance premiums
  • Fuel charge
  • Luxury tax
  • Nova Scotia Workers' Compensation Board payments
  • Part XIII withholding tax (non-resident)
  • Softwood lumber products export charge
  • Underused housing tax
Trusts
  • Trust income tax (T3)
Payments we do not accept online through your bank or credit union
Individuals
  • Alberta credits repayment
  • Climate Action Incentive (CAI) repayment
  • Ontario credits repayment
  • TFSA amount owing
Businesses
  • Canada Emergency Rent Subsidy (CERS) repayment
  • Garnishment on behalf of an employee
  • Information Return (RZ), T5, RRSP, TFSA, etc. penalty
Charities
  • Re-register a charity

If you can't use this method or want to pay in another way, find options for your situation by answering a few questions about what you are paying.

How to pay the amount you owe

Make a payment for yourself, on behalf of someone else, or for a business through your bank or credit union's online banking site or mobile banking app. Most banks or credit unions also let you set up a payment to be made on a future date.

Make your payment

  • For individuals

    1. Check if your bank or credit union supports online payments to the CRA for individuals
      • Participating bank or credit union
        • ATB Online
        • BMO
        • Canadian Western Bank
        • CIBC
        • Desjardins
        • EQ Bank
        • HSBC
        • KEB Canada
        • Laurentian Bank
        • National Bank
        • President’s Choice
        • RBC Royal Bank
        • Scotiabank1
        • Tangerine
        • TD Canada Trust1
        • Alberta Credit Unions1
        • Atlantic Canada Credit Unions1

        1 You can pay non-resident withholding tax (Part XIII tax) at these financial institutions

    2. Sign in to your bank or credit union's online banking service for individuals.
    3. Make sure you select the correct option under "add a payee" so the CRA applies your payment correctly. Common options may include:
      Pay your tax owing before assessment

      Select an option such as:

      • "CRA (revenue) – 20XX tax return"
      Pay an assessed tax owing

      Select an option such as:

      • "CRA (revenue) – tax amount owing"
      Pay a tax instalment

      Quarterly instalment due dates: March 15, June 15, Sept. 15, Dec. 15

      Select an option such as:

      • "CRA (revenue) – tax instalment"
      Repay benefits and credits

      Select an option such as:

      • "GST/HST credit repayment"
      • "Canada Child Benefit (CCB) repayment"
      Repay COVID-19 benefits

      Select an option such as:

      • "CRA (revenue) – Canada emergency benefit repayment"
    4. Enter your 9-digit social insurance number as your account number.

  • For businesses

    1. Check if your bank or credit union supports online payments to the CRA for businesses
      • Participating bank or credit union
        • ATB Financial
        • Bank of America
        • BMO
        • Canadian Western Bank
        • CIBC
        • Citibank
        • Desjardins
        • J.P. Morgan
        • KEB Canada
        • Laurentian Bank
        • Meridian Credit Union
        • National Bank
        • RBC Royal Bank
        • Scotiabank
        • TD Canada Trust
        • Alberta Credit Union
        • Atlantic Canada Credit Unions

        Many of these banks and credit unions provide business customers with non-resident payment options.

    2. Sign in to your bank or credit union's tax filing service or online banking service for businesses. Check with your bank or credit union if you can't find business payment options.
    3. Make sure you select the correct option under "add a payee" so the CRA applies your payment correctly. Common options may include:
      Pay corporation tax

      Select an option such as:

      • "Federal – Corporation Tax Payments – TXINS"
      • "Federal - Corporation Income Tax Balance Due -- TXBAL"
      Remit GST/HST

      Select an option such as:

      • "Federal – GST/HST Payment – GST-P (GST-P)"
      • "Federal - GST/HST Return and Payment -- GST34 -- (GST34)"
      Remit payroll

      Select an option such as:

      • "Federal Payroll Deductions – Regular/Quarterly – EMPTX – (PD7A)"
      • "Federal Payroll Deductions – Threshold 1 – EMPTX – (PD7A)"
      • "Federal Payroll Deductions – Threshold 2 – EMPTX – (PD7A)"
      Repay COVID subsidy overpayments

      Select an option such as:

      • "Federal - Canada emergency wage subsidy repayment"
      • "CRA Wage subsidies and hiring program repayment -- REPAY -- (CEWS & CRHP repayment / Remb. SSUC & PEREC)"
      Pay a customs tax debt

      You may only pay a customs tax debt in collections online with a bank or credit union with an importer's business number (RM account).

      To find out what payee to select for your financial institution:

      • Go to Canada Border Services Agency
      • Scroll down and click on “Expand All”
      • Under “Option 1 - Online Banking” note the payee names

      From your bank or credit union’s website:

      • Select the appropriate payee name for your financial institution
      • Add your 9 digit Business Number with your RM identification (for example 123456789 RM0005)

    4. Follow the instructions which would include entering your 15-digit business number as your CRA account number.

Processing time

  • Considered as paid: the same or next business day (varies based on your bank or credit union)
  • Updated in your CRA account up to 3 business days after the payment is received

You can confirm if we received your payment.

If your payment is still not showing after 3 business days, you can resolve your payment issue.
